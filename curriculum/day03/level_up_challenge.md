# 🏆 Day 3 Level Up Challenge: The AI Mad Libs Generator 🎭

**Goal:** Use your String and f-String skills to build a game that creates hilarious, random stories by plugging user data into templates.

---

### 📋 The Mission
You are the scriptwriter for a new comedy movie. You need to create a Python program that takes 5 random words from the user and turns them into a "Movie Teaser" on the screen.

### 🛠️ Step 1: The Collection
Create 5 variables that ask for:
- `hero_name`
- `scary_animal`
- `superpower`
- `funny_object`
- `action_verb`

### 🛠️ Step 2: The Template
Create one long f-string that uses all these variables to tell a short story.
*Example:* `f"In a world where {hero_name} was the only one with the power of {superpower}..."`

### 🛠️ Step 3: The Big Reveal
Use `print()` to show the final "Movie Script" to the screen. 
**Challenge:** Use `.upper()` for the `hero_name` so it looks like a real script!

---

### 🌟 Ultra Mission for "Flash" Students
Ask **Antigravity Gemini**:
> "I am building a Mad Libs game. Can you give me a funny story template that is 4 sentences long? Write it as a Python f-string that has at least 5 blanks inside curly braces `{}` for me to fill in!"

### 🌟 The "Real World" Mission
Imagine you are building **Netflix**. Create an f-string that welcomes the user and tells them how many movies are in their "Watch Later" list. 
Example: `f"Welcome back {user}! You have {count} movies waiting for you! ✨"`

---

### 🕵️ Why are we doing this?
In the professional world, this is called **"Template Rendering."** 
When you get an email from **Spotify** saying "Your 2024 Wrapped is here!", they aren't typing your name manually. They are using an f-string template to send millions of customized messages at once. You just mastered the technology that powers every social media notification in the world!
