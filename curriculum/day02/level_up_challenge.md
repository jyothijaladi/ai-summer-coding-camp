# 🏆 Day 2 Level Up Challenge: The Combat Simulator ⚔️

**Goal:** Master math and comparisons by building a turn-based combat calculator for an RPG game.

---

### 📋 The Mission
You are building the internal math engine for a new fantasy game. You need to calculate damage and check if the hero survives the round!

### 🛠️ Step 1: The Character Stats
Create these variables:
- `hero_hp = 100`
- `hero_attack = 25`
- `monster_hp = 100`
- `monster_attack = 10`

### 🛠️ Step 2: The Attack (Subtraction)
Calculate the new `monster_hp` after the hero attacks. 
*Hint:* `monster_hp = monster_hp - hero_attack`.
Print the new monster HP with a cool message!

### 🛠️ Step 3: The Counter-Attack (Math & Logic)
1. Subtract the `monster_attack` from the `hero_hp`.
2. **Check the Stats:** Print whether the `hero_hp` is still greater than 0.
3. **Compare Power:** Print whether the `hero_attack` is greater than the `monster_attack`.

---

### 🌟 Ultra Mission for "Flash" Students
Ask **Antigravity Gemini**:
> "I am building a combat game in Python. I want the monster's attack to be **Random** between 5 and 20. Can you help me write the code for a random monster attack?"

### 🌟 The "Real World" Mission
Create a variable `distance_to_enemy = 50`. 
Calculate what the distance is after moving **10 units closer**. 
Print whether `distance_to_enemy` is now less than or equal to 40. 
*This simulates how games like **Among Us** know when you are close enough to interact with a task!*

---

### 🕵️ Why are we doing this?
Every 3D game in the world (from **Minecraft** to **Roblox**) is just a giant math machine. Every time you move, fall, or jump, the computer is running **subtraction, addition, and comparisons** millions of times per second. You just built the core logic of a professional level combat system!
