# 🔢 Day 2: The Math Lab & Smart Numbers

**Time:** 9:00 AM - 12:00 PM
**Daily Goal:** Learn how computers calculate and how they compare two numbers.

---

## 🕒 9:00 AM – 9:40 AM: Concept 1 – The Calculator Lab ➕
*Python is the world's most powerful calculator.*

1.  **Basic Operators:**
    - Adding `+`, Subtracting `-`, Multiplying `*`, and Dividing `/`.
2.  **Order of Operations:**
    - Just like in school: PEMDAS! (Parentheses first).
3.  **The AI Connection:**
    - Ask Gemini: "I have 15 cookies and 4 friends. Write a Python snippet that calculates how many cookies each friend gets and how many are left over." (Introduce `%` as a bonus!).

---

## 🕒 9:40 AM – 10:00 AM: 🍎 Brain Break (20 mins)

---

## 🕒 10:00 AM – 10:40 AM: Concept 2 – Comparing Numbers ⚖️
*The "True/False" test.*

1.  **The Comparisons:**
    - Greater than `>`, Less than `<`, and the "Royal Equal" `==`.
    - Note: Explain why we use `==` to compare but `=` to assign.
2.  **The Result:**
    - Every comparison results in an answer: `True` or `False`.
3.  **The AI Connection:**
    - Task: Ask Gemini: "Write 3 comparison expressions that equal 'True' and 3 that equal 'False'."

---

## 🕒 10:40 AM – 11:00 AM: 🥤 Mini-Break / Buffer

---

## 🕒 11:00 AM – 12:00 PM: 🏝️ The Sandbox Exploration (Level 2)
*Goal: Build a "Scoreboard Validator".*

**Challenges for the Students:**
1.  **The Math Wizard:** Create two variables, `a` and `b`. Use Python to print the result of `(a + b) * a`.
2.  **The Referee:** Create a variable named `high_score` (100) and `my_score` (85). Write a comparison that prints whether `my_score` is greater than `high_score`.
3.  **The AI Boss Fight:** 
    - Open Antigravity Gemini.
    - Prompt: "I am building a game. I need to check if the `player_age` is 13 or older. Write a line of code that compares `player_age` to 13 and prints the result."

---

### 💡 Teacher's Tip:
*If a student gets a result that doesn't make sense (like `3.333333335`), explain that computers are sometimes "too specific" with numbers. This is a great intro to why we sometimes need to "round" things!*
