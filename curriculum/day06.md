# 🔗 Day 6: The Logical Detective

**Time:** 9:00 AM - 12:00 PM
**Daily Goal:** Learn how to combine multiple conditions using `and`, `or`, and `not`.

---

## 🕒 9:00 AM – 9:40 AM: Concept 1 – The "Both" and "Either" Operators (`and` / `or`) 🔗
*Learning how to check multiple things at once.*

1.  **The `and` Operator:** 
    - Both conditions must be True. 
    - *Example:* "If I have money AND the store is open, I can buy candy."
2.  **The `or` Operator:**
    - Only one condition needs to be True.
    - *Example:* "If it's Saturday OR it's Sunday, I don't have school."
3.  **The AI Connection:**
    - Ask Gemini: "Write an `if` statement that checks if a player has a `key` and their `health` is greater than 0."

---

## 🕒 9:40 AM – 10:00 AM: 🍎 Brain Break (20 mins)

## 🕒 10:00 AM – 10:40 AM: Concept 2 – The "Opposite" Operator (`not`) 🧠
*Flipping the truth.*

1.  **The `not` Operator:**
    - It turns True into False and False into True.
    - *Example:* `if not is_raining: print("Let's go outside!")`
2.  **Order of Logic:**
    - Explain that `not` happens before `and`, and `and` happens before `or`. (Use parentheses `()` to be safe!).
3.  **The AI Connection:**
    - Task: Ask Gemini: "I have a variable `is_game_over = False`. How do I use the `not` operator to print 'The game is still going!'?"

### 💡 Teacher's Tip:
*Logical operators are the key to building complex games. Remind the kids that computers are very literal—if you say `if name == "Alex" or "Bob":`, Python will get confused. You have to say `if name == "Alex" or name == "Bob":`!*
